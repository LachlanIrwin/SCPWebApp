Item #: SCP-004

Object Class: Euclid

Special Containment Procedures:

When handling items SCP-004-2 through SCP-004-13, proper procedure is vital. Unauthorized removal of keys from the testing area is grounds for immediate termination.

Level 1 clearance is required for basic access to SCP-004-1; Level 4 clearance is required for use of SCP-004-2 to -13.

Description:

SCP-004 consists of an old wooden barn door (SCP-004-1) and a set of twelve rusted steel keys (SCP-004-2 through SCP-004-13). The door itself is the entrance to an abandoned factory.

Each key fits into SCP-004-1, but opening it with the wrong key results in catastrophic effects, including dismemberment and disappearance.

Chronological History:

07/02/1949: A group of juveniles trespassing near the factory discover SCP-004-1 and its keys. One individual goes missing after opening the door.

07/03/1949: Local authorities find the severed hand of the missing individual several kilometers away. The SCP Foundation takes over the investigation.

08/02/1949: Testing begins. Only two out of twelve test subjects survive after attempting to enter SCP-004-1 with different keys.

Space-Time Anomalies:

Personnel exiting SCP-004-1 report time loss. Some remains of missing individuals have reappeared decades later, seemingly untouched by time.

Additional Information:

Testing suggests SCP-004-2 through -13 may open doors to other dimensions. The exact nature of these realities remains unknown.
